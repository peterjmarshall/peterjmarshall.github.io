About My Research

My research investigates how the brain represents and understands the body, particularly during early development. I explore how infants and young children develop "body maps" in the brain - neural representations that help us understand our own bodies and those of others. This work connects to broader questions about embodied cognition: how our physical experiences shape the way we think and learn.

Recently, my research has expanded to include human-robot interaction, examining how children perceive and interact with social robots. This work explores fascinating questions about embodiment in artificial agents and has important implications for designing robots that can effectively support child development and learning.
