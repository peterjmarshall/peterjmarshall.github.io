About Me

I am a researcher and teacher with interests in questions at the interface of development, embodiment, and neuroscience.

I am Professor of Psychology and Neuroscience at Temple University in Philadelphia (USA). For my official university page click here, and for a university-hosted site that I maintain, click here.

This personal page houses links to a set of Open Educational Resources (OER) that I developed.

Open Educational Resources

  • About Development
    This collection of interconnected essays explores questions about development and change across biological and psychological domains.
  • Enacting Life
    The materials on this site explore how living things create meaning through their active engagement with the world.
  • Human Brains
    These writings examine questions about human development through an integrative lens that connects evolutionary, developmental, and cultural perspectives.
